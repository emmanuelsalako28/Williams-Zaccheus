💡 How I Tag and File Emails

  • By urgency: High priority items like client requests or anything with a deadline get flagged and actioned the same day.
  • By sender type: Client, internal, vendor, and finance emails each get their own folder so nothing gets lost in one giant inbox.
  • By action needed: I separate emails that need a reply from ones that just need to be read, so a client can quickly see what's actually pending.
  • Routine cleanup: Promotional and low value emails get archived or unsubscribed on a regular basis, so the inbox stays lean.

Customer Service & Cold Calling Samples

Compare Williams' approach across customer retention support and outbound lead generation campaigns.

Live Support Session (Frustrated Customer)
Customer
Hi, my order still hasn't arrived and the tracking hasn't moved in almost a week. This is really frustrating, I need this by Friday.
Williams (Support Pro)
I completely understand the frustration, waiting on something with a deadline and watching the tracking stall is not a good feeling. Let me pull up your order right now and find out exactly what's going on.
💡 De-escalation: Acknowledges the emotion first, before jumping to the fix. The customer feels heard, not processed.
Williams (Support Pro)
Thanks for your patience. I can see the shipment reached the regional facility on Monday but hasn't been scanned since, which usually points to a carrier delay rather than a lost package. I don't want to just guess though, so I'm opening a trace request with the carrier right now.
💡 Transparency: Gives a real update instead of a generic "I'm looking into it," which builds trust.
Customer
Okay, but what if it doesn't arrive by Friday?
Williams (Support Pro)
Fair question. Here's what I'll do. I'll have an update on the trace within 24 hours. If it looks like Friday is at risk, I'll go ahead and process either an expedited replacement or a full refund, whichever you'd prefer, so you're not left stuck at the last minute.
💡 Assurance: Offers a concrete backup plan with a timeline, so the customer isn't left uncertain.
Customer
That actually makes me feel a lot better. Thank you.
Williams (Support Pro)
Of course. I've made a note on your account so if you message back in, whoever picks it up will already have the full context and you won't have to explain everything again. I'll follow up personally within 24 hours either way.
💡 Continuity: Leaves a note on the account so the customer never has to repeat their story.
Outbound Lead Qualification Script Objective: Qualify genuine need and book a follow up meeting.
Williams: "Hi, good afternoon, this is Williams calling on behalf of [Company]. Am I catching you at an okay time, or would it be better to call back?" Respect: Opens by respecting the prospect's time rather than launching straight into a pitch.
Prospect: "I've got a couple of minutes, what's this about?"
Williams: "Appreciate that. I'll keep it short. We work with businesses like yours to help with [core offer]. I'm not calling to sell you anything today, I just want to ask a couple of quick questions to see if this is even relevant for you. Sound fair?" Consent: States clearly and early that the call is not a hard sell, which lowers resistance.
Prospect: "Sure, go ahead."
Williams: "How are you currently handling [relevant process] right now, in house, through another provider, or still figuring it out?" Discovery: Uses an open question to surface the real pain point instead of guessing at it.
Prospect: "Honestly, we're doing it ourselves and it's a bit of a mess."
Williams: "That's really common, and it's exactly the kind of thing we help with. Rather than me pitching you over the phone, would it make sense to grab 15 minutes with our team so they can show you how it works and you can decide if it's worth pursuing? I've got Tuesday afternoon or Thursday morning open, whichever works better for you?" Call to Action: Moves straight to booking once genuine interest is confirmed, without over explaining or losing momentum.
Prospect: "Thursday morning works."
Williams: "Perfect, I'll get that booked and send a confirmation right after this call. Thanks for your time today, have a great rest of your afternoon."
